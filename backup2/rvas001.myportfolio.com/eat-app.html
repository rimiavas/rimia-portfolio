Eat-app


Context
Eats is developed for individuals with dietary restrictions or picky eaters who may have difficulty finding restaurants or takeout options that cater to their specific needs. Currently, there are no apps available in the UK that cater to the needs of individuals with dietary restrictions. While there are apps available for those with food allergies, they do not have a feature that allows users to order takeaways or provide recommendations for restaurants that meet their specific requirements.

Eats aims to fill this gap by providing a platform for users to easily find restaurants and takeout options that cater to their specific dietary needs. The app will provide a list of key dietary categories for users to choose from when they sign up, including food allergies and intolerances, specific dietary needs such as veganism or pregnancy, and religious restrictions. 

The app will also allow users to customise their preferences and provide recommendations for restaurants that meet their specific requirements. This will provide users with the assurance to enter a restaurant without worrying about whether the establishment will serve food that satisfies their needs.

Purpose/aim of the app
the main purpose of the app is to provide a platform for individuals with dietary restrictions or picky eaters to easily find restaurants and takeout options that cater to their specific needs. The app aims to fulfil the need for a resource that allows these individuals to find options that meet their dietary requirements and provide them with the assurance to enter a restaurant without worrying about whether the establishment will serve food that satisfies their needs.

To achieve this, the app will provide a list of key dietary categories for users to choose from when they sign up, including food allergies and intolerances, specific dietary needs such as veganism or pregnancy, and religious restrictions. The app will also allow users to customise their preferences and provide recommendations for restaurants that meet their specific requirements. This will provide users with the ability to easily search for options that cater to their needs and

The app will have the following functional requirements:

• A user registration and login system that allows users to create an account and securely store their dietary preferences and restrictions.
• A search feature that allows users to find restaurants and takeout options based on their specific dietary requirements.
• A rating and review system that allows users to provide feedback on their experience with a restaurant, as well as see ratings and reviews from other users.
• A verification system that allows verifiers to confirm that a restaurant complies with regulations for accommodating guests with dietary restrictions.
• A messaging system that allows users to contact restaurants directly for more information about their menu and any special requests.
• A delivery integration system that allows users to easily order takeout from participating restaurants, either through the restaurant's own delivery service or through a third-party delivery partner.
• A notifications system that alerts users to new restaurants and deals that match their dietary preferences.

Primary stakeholders:

• Potential clients (main categories):
o Individual with food intolerances and allergies
o Individual's special dietary needs (vegan, vegetarians, pregnancy)
o a follower of religion (halal, Kosher etc)
o A picky eater

These are the main users of the app, who are individuals with dietary restrictions or picky eaters. The app will provide a platform for these users to easily find restaurants and takeout options that cater to their specific needs and give them the assurance that the establishments they choose will meet their dietary requirements.

• Verifiers from the area (external) (main categories):

These are individuals or organizations that use the app to check whether establishments comply with regulations to accommodate guests with dietary restrictions. This will help to ensure that restaurants are meeting the needs of their customers and providing a safe and inclusive environment for all.

• Moderators:

These are individuals responsible for moderating the reviews and ratings provided by users on the app, as well as the input of verifiers. This will help to ensure the accuracy and reliability of the information provided on the app.

Secondary stakeholders:

• HMC (halal food Certification):

If a restaurant claims that their food is halal, they must provide proof of certification from the HMC, which guarantees that all the meat and goods they purchase are halal.

• Kosher certification agency:

If a restaurant claims that their food is kosher, they must provide proof of certification from a kosher certification agency, which confirms that the ingredients, manufacturing site, and production process comply with kosher standards.

• Food Standard Agency (FSA):

Restaurants must ensure that they comply with the regulations set by the FSA, which includes labelling for allergens and providing customers with required information. The app Eats may provide a platform for restaurants to easily access and comply with these regulations.
